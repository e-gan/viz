Subtheme: Transit-Oriented Development

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. What is the current density of housing around MBTA communities?Who lives in the Greater Boston Area and what sort of zoning and housing do they occupy?
  2. Which communities and demographics would be impacted by high-density housing in MBTA communities, especially in places which oppose adoption of TOD? The subtheme reading states that there has been differences in adoption across different towns. For example, inner Boston has smoothly adopted TOD, but Milton opposes it.
  3. Can we tell the reasoning for opposition using the demographic makeup of the town? Perhaps there are functional reasons for why a community may not want to implement TOD. The reading suggests that suburban areas may oppose TOD.

Data

I used the MBTA stops collapsed dataset and the single family zoning dataset. I aggregated the latter over municipalities, summing relevant columns and calculating percentages based on aggregate numbers per municipality. It would have been nice to have data for each MBTA stop with the half-mile buffer, but since the data was given at the municipality granularity, this was the level I chose to work with.

Methods

I used Bokeh, an interactive Python library for plotting. I started with the municipality regions and tried to overlay it over the OpenStreetMap tile over the Greater Boston Metropolitan Area, along with the MBTA stops. I noticed that these datasets use different coordinate systems, so I converted all to Web Mercator (WGS84) using the package pyproj. To get bounds for the figures in Web Mercator coordinates, I used the online converter XY Converter.

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

The single-family zoning of residential land is clearly one major obstacle of transit-oriented development, as it restricts the density of housing in MBTA communities. Using Milton as a case study of opposition, we see a large percentage of land use is for single-family housing, despite most households being categorized as non-family. There is clearly some friction between the intention of the zoning restriction of housing types and their in-practice usage. Age could also play a role in the opposition.
